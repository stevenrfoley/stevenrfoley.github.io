I’m a syntactician, psycholinguist, and fieldworker, currently working as a postdoctoral researcher and lecturer at Princeton’s Program in Linguistics. I graduated from UC Santa Cruz in 2020. Here’s a pdf of my CV.

My research focus is morphosyntax, especially case, which I investigate from both formal–theoretical and experimental angles. I’m interested in how comprehenders leverage case morphology during on-line syntactic processing, and in the grammatical & extra-grammatical factors that shape crosslinguistic case-alignment typology. My primary muse is Georgian, a language from the small South Caucasian family with an especially complex morphosyntactic grammar. Ongoing theoretical work examines the syntactic properties of Georgian dative subjects; my next experiments will plumb garden paths stemming from the incremental ambiguity of the language's dative case.

I’ve also worked on phi-agreement, pronominal cliticization, and relative clauses. At UCSC, I was involved in several collaborative projects investigating Santiago Laxopa Zapotec and its rich grammar of animacy.

A life-long hobby of mine is visual art — take a look at some of my drawings and collages. I also have a fledgling YouTube channel, where I plan to post more linguistics content in the future.

I prefer that you refer to me with they/them pronouns, but I don’t mind he/him.
